<b>C'hi++</b> is an attempt to explain the meaning of Existence in the terms of computer science.
This blend of spirituality and technology shows up both in the name *C'hi++* &mdash; a play on words between the Japanese term *C'hi* and the programming language called *C++* &mdash;, and in the way the evolution of the discipline will be managed: the same way *open-source* software is managed.<br />
The precepts of C'hi++ are laid out in this very manual.
All texts, images, and data related to C'hi++ are collected in a
<a  title="Go to the GitHub project" target="github" href="{{ site.url_github }}">repository on GitHub</a>;
if you want more information about this project, you can find it there, in the files:
<a href="https://github.com/TacunIT/chi-plus-plus" target="github">README</a> and
<a href="https://github.com/TacunIT/chi-plus-plus/blob/master/MANIFEST.md" target="github">MANIFEST</a>.

---

C'hi++ is neither science nor religion, even though it sometimes pretends to be one or the other.
C'hi++ is myth, and mythology has often blended the supernatural with the highest level of scientific or technological development of its time.
In antiquity, we attributed a divine counterpart to the lightning that generated fire, to the motion of the stars, to the weather.
More recently, Poe, in *Eureka*, hypothesized that the Universe is the result of the opposing action of Gravity and Electricity.
At the start of the last century, finally, the Futurists mythologized the symbols of technological progress &mdash; speed, propellers, airplanes, automobiles &mdash;, making them the subject of their art.
So it's nothing strange that at the start of this millennium someone would attempt a comparison between the known rules of software and the unknown rules of the Universe we live in.

---

C'hi++ does not present itself as an absolute and definitive explanation of the Meaning of Existence, but as an incomplete and perfectible model.
This is precisely why it has a version number, like software: so that it can evolve over time, the same way software does.

Just as the mathematical models used for meteorology don't capture the full complexity of the atmosphere's air-mass movements, but still provide reliable forecasts, so C'hi++ does not offer a complete and accurate description of what is, was, and will be, but it can offer a non-dogmatic answer to some questions about our life.

---

For C'hi++, every sentient being is one of the neurons of a brain (the Universe) which it helps shape through what it learns over the course of its existences.
If we recognize our mistakes as such, it's possible we won't repeat them in the future; otherwise we'll keep making them, generating "painful" forms of the Universe.
For this reason, according to C'hi++, "*the meaning of Life is debugging*", because only by analyzing and correcting our errors, the same way it's done with software, can we improve the "shape" of the Universe and, as a consequence, our existences.
In other words, Heaven and Hell are not elsewhere: they are just two possible states of the Universe, and it is we, through our actions and our intentions, who decide which of the two we want to live in.
